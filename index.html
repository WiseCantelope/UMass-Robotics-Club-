Welcome to the UMass Robotics Club

The UMass Robotics Club is a welcoming space for all students—no matter their major or experience level—to explore the world of robotics. Our projects are hands-on and cross-disciplinary, giving members opportunities to dive into mechanical design, software development, electronics, and artistic avenues that go into building robots.

Along the way, students sharpen critical thinking skills and gain practical experience that translates directly into careers and everyday problem-solving. Whether you’re in the College of Engineering, CICS, or simply curious about robotics, the club is an incredible way to apply classroom knowledge, build new skills, and connect with peers who share your interests.

We’re proud to be a College of Engineering JSES, a CICS club, and an official RSO.

This Year’s Teams

Research Team

In partnership with the Dynamic & Autonomous Robotics Systems Lab, the Research Team is tackling the challenge of designing a lightweight quadruped robot. This modular platform advances members’ knowledge of robotics while contributing to ongoing campus research.

Ant Weight BattleBot Competition

This beginner-friendly project invites students of all skill levels to design, build, and compete with their own small-scale battle bots. Supported by CICS, the competition provides starter kits, workshops, and access to a competition-grade arena.
